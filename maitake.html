MAITAKE MUSHROOMS

What are maitake mushrooms?

Maitake mushrooms are a type of mushrooms native to China, the northeastern part of Japan, and North America. They are widely eaten in Japan and their popularity in western cuisine is growing. Maitake is the Japanese name meaning "dancing mushroom", but the grifola frondosa is also known as hen of the woods, ram's head or sheep's head. It is typically found in late summer to early autumn.

What are the benefits?

Maitake mushrooms are said to offer a wide range of health benefits. They are rich in antioxidants, beta-glucans, vitamins B and C, copper, potassium, fiber, minerals and amino acids. Early research suggests maitake mushrooms may help stabilise blood sugar levels and lower cholesterol levels, as well as boost immune function.

How do you eat them?

You can replace other mushroom types with maitake in your favourite mushroom dish, as simple as that, though be mindful that maitake have a strong, earthy taste. Maitake may also be taken as a liquid concentrate or in dry form in capsules.
